Hindu Marriage Act Section 13 | Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 | Source: https://www.indiacode.nic.in/

A marriage may be dissolved by a decree of divorce on grounds such as:
(a) cruelty,
(b) desertion for a continuous period of not less than two years,
(c) adultery,
(d) conversion to another religion,
(e) incurable mental disorder,
(f) venereal disease,
(g) renunciation of the world,
(h) not heard of as alive for seven years.

Either spouse may file for divorce based on these grounds.
