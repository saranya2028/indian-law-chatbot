CrPC Section 41 | Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 | Source: https://www.indiacode.nic.in/

Any police officer may, without an order from a Magistrate and without a warrant, arrest any person:
(a) who has committed a cognizable offence,
(b) who is in possession of stolen property,
(c) who obstructs a police officer in the execution of duty,
(d) who is a proclaimed offender,
(e) who commits a cognizable offence in the presence of the officer,
(f) against whom credible information exists of involvement in a cognizable offence.

Arrest should be made only when necessary and with recorded reasons.
